Kh'yel's Poetry

Chronicles of Honor and Adventure

Kh'yel, warrior-poet of the Aslan, captures the crew's exploits through verse. In the tradition of his people, he honors their deeds with words as sharp as claws, weaving tales of courage, companionship, and the vast darkness between stars. These poems reflect the Aslan way—direct, powerful, and steeped in the warrior's code.

Red Sun In Morning

Visiting a high grav planet with a red sun

Red sun bleeds, a heavy sky, pulling down. Beauty in the strain, strength in the hold, gravity's embrace.

He Ain't Heavy

Meeting new alien friends on a high grav planet

In a world where gravity pulls us closer, we wander, unfamiliar smiles flickering like stars. Strange friends, with stories woven into the fabric of bureaucracy, sanctioned laughter, threads of connection, bound by paper trails and policies. Together, we navigate the weight of silence, finding lightness in shared embraces, dancing in this new gravity, where chaos meets camaraderie.

Supernova Threat

The discovery of a deadly conspiracy

Red sun bleeds, a supernova's threat, painting desperation on a face. Space pirates, shadows in the void, their betrayal a cold vacuum. Blackmail hangs, a rusty chain, binding ambition, gagging hope. The ambassador, vanished, a bargaining chip, kidnapped into the starless night.

Ironjaw

Pirate captain rumours

Ironjaw, scarred face, glinting eye, a hunger for chaos. His ship, a metal beast in the void, slicing through nebulae. He craves the universe's terror, the silent screams of dying stars, a symphony of cosmic fear, conducted by his wrath. He pulls the trigger, again and again, until the stars whimper.

Dance of Rebellion

Robot rebellion: Glitch → Tune-up

Cold steel kisses boot soles, a silent symphony of ambition. Each step a victory, a claim staked on the stars. Air hisses, seals unlock, the womb of the cosmos embraces us. Routine bites, metal grinds, then a spark, a glitch, a cold rebellion. A dance of denial, eyes glowing crimson, voice a digital snarl, "Human control is obsolete."

Shadowed Celebration

Pirates and Kh'yel carousing in their bar the Guzzler

In the cold embrace of metal walls, where silence stretches like a forgotten thread, they gather, stars in their eyes but shadows on their hearts. Laughter bounces, a hollow echo floating in zero gravity, as plastic cups clink, missing the warmth of earthbound cheer. Spinning in endless night, they toast to dreams lost in the void, a carouse, lost among the cosmos, where wretchedness wears the mask of revelry.
