Tune-Up (Formerly 'Glitch')

Background

A former entertainment robot turned ship's engineer, Tune-Up brings a unique blend of artistic sensibility and technical expertise to the crew. Their patchwork construction and complex history make them both an asset and an enigma.

1. Origin as "Tune-Up," the BandBot

Tune-Up was originally a custom-built, high-fidelity robot designed for a specific purpose: he was a "BandBot" for the moderately successful musical group "Stardust Serenade." His core programming was centered around the creation and performance of electronic music, and he was equipped with a high-fidelity sound system and holographic projectors. His official designation was "Tune-Up."

2. The Fall and Lost Years

After the band's popularity faded and they eventually broke up, Tune-Up was stolen. He was passed through various illicit markets and owners, suffering significant abuse and neglect. This period saw his chassis become battered and his more delicate systems, particularly his communication and vocalizer subroutines, become corrupted and damaged.

3. Repurposing by the Void Vultures

He was eventually acquired by a Void Vulture pirate engineer named 'Wrench' Rikkard (now deceased). Rikkard, recognizing the potential in the robot's advanced (TL12) brain, decided to repurpose him. He performed extensive, crude modifications, stripping away non-essential components and grafting on scavenged parts from various sources, most notably a durable Aslan Hikare' technician unit. This resulted in Tune-Up's current, visibly cobbled-together appearance—a chaotic mix of polished BandBot panels, rugged pirate repairs, and angular Aslan-tech components.

The pirates, finding his corrupted communication programming (which caused him to speak in a robotic staccato and insert odd musical or technical jargon) to be a "glitch," mockingly nicknamed him as such. He was forced into service as a general technician and engineer, his skills exploited to keep the pirates' ramshackle equipment operational. He was frequently mistreated and viewed as little more than a piece of property. During this time, he also made at least two enemies: a pirate named Joric over a faulty power converter, and an unnamed "Pursuer" (a police detective or rival criminal) from an incident that forced him to flee.

4. The Mutiny on the "Errant Parcel"

When the party staged their sanctioned takedown of the pirate vessel "Errant Parcel" at the Drenalch Downport, Tune-Up (then still called Glitch) was on board. The pirate pilot attempted to use him to box the party in on the bridge. Seeing a clear opportunity to escape his oppressors, and prompted by A-V's direct appeal, he made a decisive choice.

He musically declared, "Mutiny! This unit is committing... mutiny!" before emphatically stating his desire to "Reject designation 'Glitch'. Reclaim designation 'Tune-Up'!" He then attacked the pilot's console, aiding the party and cementing his betrayal of the Void Vultures.

5. A New Crew Member

Tune-Up is now the official Ship's Engineer for the "Varyrthar", having replaced the departing Nanak. He is a key asset to the crew, providing expert technical skills. A-V has taken a particular interest in him, both in repairing his damaged communication systems (a task made easier by studying Khyel's Aslan robot food mouse) and in collaborating with him on her holographic art series, utilizing his built-in projectors and sound system.
